This research investigates the transformative impact of social media engagement on the formation and strengthening of brand equity in contemporary digital markets. Grounded in established frameworks like Aaker’s Brand Equity Model and Keller’s Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) framework, the study conceptualizes social media engagement as a multidimensional construct involving cognitive, emotional, and behavioral interactions. By analyzing data from 120 active social media users, the project employs descriptive-explanatory research design and statistical modeling—including Pearson correlation and multiple regression—to quantify how digital interactions such as likes, shares, and comments translate into measurable brand value across dimensions like awareness, associations, perceived quality, and loyalty.  
The empirical findings confirm a statistically significant and positive association between engagement intensity and overall brand equity. Specifically, the results demonstrate that active digital participation serves as a strategic driver for enhancing brand recall and building deeper emotional connections, which in turn elevates perceived credibility and long-term consumer loyalty. Beyond theoretical validation, this work offers actionable managerial insights, underscoring that brands should prioritize engagement depth and interactive storytelling over mere reach to achieve a sustainable competitive advantage. This project effectively bridges the gap between digital marketing analytics and strategic brand management, positioning social media engagement as a vital intangible asset for modern organizations.
